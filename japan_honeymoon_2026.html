Shopping List

Tap any item to mark it bought. Add your own below each category.

Bags & Accessories
Luxury bag / purse
Ginza is your best bet — Chanel, Louis Vuitton, Gucci all have flagship stores. Also check Isetan Shinjuku luxury floor.
Vintage bags
Tokyo has incredible vintage luxury — try Komehyo Shinjuku and Brand Off Ginza for pre-owned designer at great prices.
Clothing
Vintage clothing
Super Bookoff Bazar for thrift. Shimokitazawa district is Tokyo's vintage mecca — dense with curated secondhand stores, well worth a half-day.
Japanese clothing / fashion
Isetan Shinjuku for high-end Japanese designers. Harajuku / Omotesando for everything from streetwear to luxury.
Skincare & Makeup
@cosme TOKYO — Harajuku flagship
THE place to start. 3 floors, 25,000 products from 600 brands ranked by real Japanese consumer reviews. Shows you exactly what's trending. 1 min from Harajuku Station
Isetan Shinjuku — Beauty Apothecary
Best mix of luxury and indie J-beauty brands. The Beauty Apothecary section has niche brands you won't find anywhere else. Staff are incredibly knowledgeable. Connected to Shinjuku Station
Mitsukoshi Ginza — luxury beauty hall
For premium Japanese brands like HAKUHODO brushes, Suqqu, and Episteme that you can't find at standard department stores. Ginza
Matsumoto Kiyoshi drugstore
Everywhere in Tokyo. Best for affordable cult favorites — Hada Labo, Canmake, KATE, Biore, Shiseido drugstore line. Great prices and tax-free shopping. Budget-friendly
Shiseido flagship — Ginza
The mothership. Try the latest Shiseido products, get makeup lessons and skin analysis. 1 min from Ginza Station
Ainz & Tulpe — Shinjuku
Elevated drugstore experience. Curated J-beauty + exclusive brands like Ayura and Dazzshop. Bubble tea cafe on 2nd floor. Across from Shinjuku Station East Exit
Specific products to look for
Hada Labo toner, Canmake sunscreen primer, SK-II Pitera Essence, Suqqu foundation, HAKUHODO makeup brushes, Attenir cleansing oil (Japan's #1 ranked 2025).
Chef's Knife
Japanese chef's knife — under $400
Totally doable. Best areas: Kappabashi "Kitchen Town" — Asakusa (street full of professional kitchen supply stores) and Tsukiji outer market. Look for brands like Masamoto, Misono, or Sakai Takayuki. A solid 210mm gyuto from a reputable maker runs $100–$300.
Kappabashi is 5 min walk from Senso-ji — perfect to hit on 10/27 morning
Plants & Bonsai
Bonsai
Ueno Green Club and the shops around Omiya Bonsai Village (45 min from Tokyo) are the best. Also check Shinjuku Gyoen garden shop for smaller starter bonsai. Note: bringing plants into the US requires a USDA phytosanitary certificate — buy from a shop that can provide this for international buyers.
⚠ Check US customs rules before buying — some species restricted
Bonsai tools & supplies
Easier to bring home than a live tree. Kappabashi has good tool shops. Also Tokyu Hands department store.
Vintage Golf
Vintage golf clubs / gear
Japan has an incredible vintage golf scene. 2nd Street (2ndSTREET) chain has multiple Tokyo locations with great vintage sports finds. Super Bookoff Bazar also carries sports gear. Dedicated golf resale shops cluster around Shinjuku and Ikebukuro.
Soccer Jerseys
Soccer jerseys — Japanese clubs & international
Alpen Sports Shinjuku (huge multi-floor sports store) and Sports Authority Shibuya for Japanese national team and J-League jerseys. For vintage/retro jerseys try Harajuku vintage shops and 2nd Street.
Japan national team jersey
The Samurai Blue kit is iconic — widely available at sports stores across Tokyo. ~¥10,000–¥15,000
Pokémon
Pokémon Center Tokyo DX — Ikebukuro
The flagship Pokémon store. Exclusive merch, plushies, TCG cards, clothing. You'll also get merch at PokéPark KANTO itself on 10/18. Sunshine City Mall, Ikebukuro
Pokémon TCG cards
Japanese card packs are cheaper than US versions and often have Japan-exclusive sets. Pokémon Center and Yodobashi Camera both carry them.
Everything Else
Oedo Antique Market finds (10/18)
Vintage Japanese clothing, ornaments, ceramics, ukiyo-e prints, watches. Cash only. Go early — vendors start closing at 3pm.
Omiyage (souvenir gifts)
Japanese confectionery, matcha products, sake, chopstick sets. Best selection at Narita/Haneda airport and Tokyo Station character street.
